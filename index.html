The dataset

Real data. Not fabricated.

This project is built on the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset, publicly available on Kaggle and originally published by IBM. It contains 7,043 real customer records across 33 columns including contract type, tenure, monthly charges, service usage, billing method, tech support engagement, a pre-built churn probability score, customer lifetime value, and churn reason.

Rather than inventing account names or making up numbers, the dataset was reframed in CS language — the same translation a CSM does when pulling a data export from Salesforce or Gainsight on day one. Of the 7,043 records, 5,174 are active accounts that form the working book of business. The remaining 1,869 have churned and are excluded from the dashboard since a CSM cannot save a customer who has already left.

"The best save play is the one you run before the customer decides to leave."

The tool answers one question every Monday morning: which accounts need my attention this week, and what specifically should I do about each one?

Health score methodology

Every weight has a reason

The health score is a 0 to 100 composite built from six signals. Each signal was chosen because it maps to a real CS intervention point, not because the data happened to be available. This is the distinction between a data project and a CS tool.

SignalWeightWhy it matters
Contract type25 ptsThe strongest commitment signal. A two-year contract reflects deliberate organizational investment. A month-to-month account can cancel with 30 days notice and requires active engagement regardless of other signals.
Tenure25 ptsLongevity is the best proxy for product embedding. Accounts past 24 months have survived at least one renewal decision. Under 12 months is the highest-risk window.
MRR level20 ptsHigher-value accounts tend to have more organizational stakeholders, creating natural retention forces. Low-MRR accounts on short contracts represent the highest churn probability combination.
Tech support usage15 ptsA dark account that has never contacted support is almost always non-adoption, not satisfaction. This is the earliest leading indicator of churn and the most actionable signal for a CSM.
Billing method10 ptsAutomatic payment eliminates involuntary churn from failed transactions. Manual payment accounts have a measurable uplift in churn rate unrelated to satisfaction.
Services count5 ptsEach additional service increases switching cost. An account using 5 of 6 services has deeply integrated the platform. One using 1 of 6 can leave without disruption.

40 to 69

70 to 100

Healthy and embedded. Evaluate for upsell or advocacy program.

The dataset also includes a pre-built IBM churn risk score (0 to 100) used as a second independent signal. A high health score paired with a high churn risk score flags accounts that look stable but have behavioral indicators of flight risk. These are the most urgent calls to make.

What was built

Five phases, one working tool

01

Dataset transformation

Python · pandas · IBM Telco dataset from Kaggle

  • Mapped 33 raw columns to CSM language: tenure to months active, MonthlyCharges to MRR, TotalCharges to lifetime revenue
  • Mapped contract types to plan tiers and derived renewal windows from tenure and contract length
  • Created engagement flags, billing stability signals, and service breadth scores
  • Output a clean 7,043-row CSV feeding every downstream phase
02

Health score model

Python · documented methodology

  • Built a weighted 0 to 100 health score from six signals with every weight documented and explainable
  • Assigned risk bands: Needs Attention, On Track, Expansion Ready
  • Used the IBM pre-built churn score as a second independent signal
  • Generated recommended CS plays per account based on signal combinations
03

Interactive dashboard

HTML · CSS · JavaScript · 5,174 active accounts

  • Summary bar: total accounts, MRR at risk, needs attention, expansion ready, average health score
  • Sortable account table by health score, MRR, plan tier, renewal window, churn risk
  • Sidebar navigation filtering by view, risk level, and recommended play
  • Click any account to open a deep-dive drawer with signal-by-signal breakdown and specific next actions
04

Written case study

  • Full methodology documentation with every signal weight explained in plain English
  • Five real account analyses written in CSM voice with specific plays for each

About the dataset

The IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset contains 7,043 real customer records. The dashboard displays 5,174 of those records — the active accounts only. The remaining 1,869 records belong to customers who have already churned and are intentionally excluded.

The reason is simple: a CSM's job is to prevent churn before it happens, not to manage accounts that have already cancelled. Showing churned accounts in a book-of-business tool would be misleading — there is no action a CSM can take on a customer who has already left. The 5,174 active accounts represent the real working portfolio, scored by health, tiered by risk, and prioritized by recommended play.
